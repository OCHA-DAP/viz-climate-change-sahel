A Race to Adapt:
The Climate Crisis in the Sahel

A Chadian girl’s daily journey to fetch water reflects climate-triggered shocks experienced by her community

The Sahel is a region at the frontlines of the global climate crisis. Temperatures have increased significantly in recent decades and are projected to rise another 3-6 degrees by the end of the 21st century. Unprecedented extreme climates are projected to occur as early as the 2030s.
Zara and her family were forced to relocate earlier this year after a severe drought,triggered by rising temperatures and low rainfall,struck their village in Chad’s Lac region. The resulting poor harvest meant that Zara’s family would not have enough food to last them the rest of the year. They moved to nearby XX to stay with Zara’s uncle, whose small home is located 5km from the nearest source of water.
